Our Projects

Cutting-edge innovations that shape the future of electronics and hardware engineering

Digital Clock

An Arduino-powered clock using a 16x2 LCD and DHT22 sensor to display real-time date, time, temperature, and humidity. An RTC module ensures time is kept accurately even when powered off.

Arduino Uno DHT22 Sensor RTC Module

RC Car

An ESP32 RC car is a small robotic vehicle controlled wirelessly using the ESP32 microcontroller. The ESP32 acts as the "brain" of the car, handling motor control, wireless communication (via Wi-Fi or Bluetooth), and sensor integration.

ESP32 Wireless Control Motor Control

Proximity Sensor

The system works on the principle of ultrasonic sound wave reflection (echo principle). An ultrasonic sensor like HC-SR04 sends out high-frequency sound pulses (40 kHz) through its transmitter. When these pulses hit an obstacle, they reflect back and are received by the receiver of the sensor.

HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Detection

Obstacle Avoider

The obstacle-avoiding robot is an autonomous mobile system built using an Arduino Uno as the controller. It uses a servo-mounted ultrasonic sensor (HC-SR04) to continuously scan the path ahead. When an obstacle is detected within a certain range, the Arduino processes the distance data and decides the best direction to move.

Arduino Uno Autonomous Servo Motor

Line Following Robot

A Line Follower Robot (LFR) is an autonomous robot that follows a black line on a white surface using IR sensors. The Arduino Uno acts as the brain of the system, continuously reading signals from the IR sensors and controlling the motor driver to adjust the robot's movement.

Arduino Uno IR Sensors Line Following
